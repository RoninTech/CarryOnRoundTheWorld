 CNS-SIN-BKK

We were flying with Singapore Airlines so we boarded with the usual efficiency and were quickly settled in with a glass of bubbly. We had a great meal on this segment, with some of the nicest lamb(for me) and steak(for Paul) that we had the whole trip. The flight was smooth and we landed in Singapore. We had booked into the Aerotel for 6 hours, we had just over 8 hours in total before our flight to BKK so wanted to get some rest. Up early the next morning, we had another good flight to BKK.. this time with a nice breakfast. 

We decided we’d have 4 nights in Bangkok, 3 in the city and our last night at the airport hotel to make it easier for our early morning departure. We decided to spoil ourselves and booked a 3 nights stay at the Vie, we hadn’t been since 2019. We decided to check to see if we could leave the duffle at the airport hotel since we’d be back in 3 days which would be easier than finding storage. Leaving the duffle made it easier to take the train into the city. It wasn’t a problem and we were able to head on our way. We navigated the train to Phaya Thai and then switched over to get to Ratchathewi station. We were going to get in well before check in time but figured we could drop our backpacks and run some errands. 

Luckily our room was ready and we were able to check in. A well timed comment about us excited to be back and staying with them again after years, had Mr. Paul recognized as the VIP guest of the day. This resulted in an upgrade to a suite, chocolates being delivered and a free beverage in the lounge. We dropped our bags and headed out to run some errands. We got everything managed and headed back to the hotel to relax. We found a nearby place for sushi and headed back for an early night. The next morning we enjoyed the breakfast buffet before taking off for an appointment. This had us taking new trains and seeing an area of BKK we hadn’t been to before. We headed to MBK for the afternoon to get some gifts to bring back. The malls definitely changed and we got out as quickly as we could. We headed over to CoCo Walk for dinner and back to the hotel to rest. We planned a massage and pedicure the next morning before heading back to the hotel. Paul managed to get a hair cut at a nearby salon.

We headed out that night over to Soi 11 to meet Tik and Jesse for dinner and drinks. They were in BKK before heading  back to Canada. They were leaving the day after us and basing themselves at Jesse’s parents in Alberta.  We had a really nice evening and enjoyed catching up. Some good laughs and a nice way to wind up our time in BKK. Paul arranged a late check out so the next morning, we enjoyed a nice breakfast and lunch before heading to the train for the airport. We arrived at the Hyatt Airport, checked in and dumped the bags before heading out to stretch our legs. We wanted to check where the Air Canada counter was so our early morning departure wouldn’t be stressful. We had a nice meal at the hotel and an early night. We were up to catch the 4 am shuttle and heading off on our next flights.

BKK-YVR-YXC

When we decided to head back to Canada, we found a flight with Air Canada that got us back within the shortest time frame, 18 hours. It was a direct BKK-YVR flight, a reasonable layover before we caught the early flight to YXC. It was reasonably priced for business class and a lot of people had said service had improved so we took a chance and booked it. We decided to book a return flight as we knew we’d be coming back to Chiang Mai. Since we knew where to go, check in was quite easy and we quickly made it back to the lounge. We had time to have some coffee and a snack to tide us over until we made it to the plane. We had to head over to the newly completed section of BKK, the S gates but we had plenty of time to make it over. The boarding process was smooth and we were quickly settled into our seats. The flight attendants were friendly and attentive. Our seats were comfortable and the food was decent, my steak was a bit overdone but everything else was good. 

The flight was long, I think 14 hours in the air, but mostly smooth. Neither of us got any sleep but we were able to rest, read and watch some movies. We landed on time in YVR, got the duffle, went through customs, dropped the duffle and headed off to our gate. We had a long wait in the security line up and my bag got pulled but we had enough time so no big deal. After a bit of a bumpy flight, we arrived in YXC on time. Dave, Paul’s Dad friend, had arranged that he would drive to pick us up. By the time we collected the duffle, we still hadn’t seen anyone so went outside to check, no one was there either. We waited about half an hour before we called. Turns out they had forgotten about the time change but were only about 10 minutes away. Paul’s Dad was happy to see us, we loaded in the car and Dave drove us all back to Creston. 

We put the duffle and bags in the room and went out on the back deck to have a visit. Rita was there and the four of us visited for the afternoon. Dave knew I was looking forward to cooking, so he offered his old air fryer for me to try, he dropped it off and I put it on one of the counters. Paul’s Dad got everyone some fish and chips for dinner and we had a very early night. Paul and I were both pretty shattered. Our plan was to spend the summer in Creston, helping Paul’s parents sort out some issues they were having with banking, keeping on top of the house and the yard and long term planning. We were going to spend time with Pati and John as well as heading to Calgary for a few days to handle some of our meetings and see other friends. 

The next morning was not good. Rita lost her temper about the air fryer and said some horribly nasty things to Paul. He managed to keep his cool and we moved past that incident. I went with John to get some groceries while Paul started to help Rita with the banking. Unfortunately this didn’t go well, resulting in more horribly nasty comments and Rita stating we were not welcome.  We had a quiet afternoon trying to settle things down and I made dinner for Paul, John and myself. Paul and I made the difficult decision to leave as we wouldn’t be able to help and didn’t want to aggravate the situation so changed our flights to leave the following Monday. 

The rest of the week was filled with ups and downs, denials, tears and upset. We did manage to help with a few things, had some nice visits with Paul’s Dad and got to see Pati & John for 2 dinners. Dave agreed to take us to the airport with John and we headed out. We got to the airport on time only to find out our flight had been delayed. We were supposed to leave at 7:45PM, which gave us at least 2 hours in the Signature Lounge in YVR. This was a perk of the tickets we bought and we had looked forward to enjoying one of the gourmet meals that we had read about. Unfortunately we didn’t actually depart until 9:20PM and the delay meant we had enough time to poke our nose into the lounge. We tried to get a quick dessert which wasn’t available, which was probably for the best as we had to hustle to get the rest of the way to the gate. A bit disappointing, but the important thing was we got our flight.

YXC-YVR-BKK-CNX

Service on the flight was great. With all the stress we had forgotten to order our meal so chose off the set menu. The food was still good and we enjoyed a nice glass of wine or two to relax. Paul managed to get some sleep, about 6 hours he thinks. I probably got 2 or 3. It was a bit bumpier on this flight, a lot more turbulence but we landed on time. We made it through immigration, got the duffle and went to check in for our next flight. We had to wait a few hours for our next flight to CNX. All things considered, Air Canada was better than expected and we both said we’d fly with them again on our next trip to Canada… with the caveat being that it had to be in business class. 

Paul had gotten in touch with Jen at the Siri Condominium, our usual building and we managed to arrange an apartment which would be ready the day we arrived. Ian picked us up from the airport, which was really nice. When we got to the condo, we could choose from 2 different units on either the 3rd or 4th floor. We checked them both out and the deciding factor was the higher floor and a real fridge with a separate freezer. We went for lunch with Ian before heading back to unpack and start to settle in. We crossed so many time zones, had so much stress and upset that we expect it’s going to take us a while before we get over the past two weeks.
