We know, we know… we said we weren’t going back but yet here we are heading back again. How blasé 🙄. Long story short, or the TL;DR version… got a really good deal on a van we couldn’t pass up, used mileage rewards for some nice “bidness class” flights which included an A-380 and booked 3 months. Skip the next two paragraphs if you are good with that!

The longer version is that I had started following Let’s Go Motorhomes on “the socials” after the 2023's trip. Sitting in Calgary, I happened to see a post for a new van that was on sale for $99/day which had me perusing the details. Paul happened to see this and was also curious, so we started looking into it. The van in question is [The Tourer](https://www.letsgomotorhomes.com.au/vehicles/campervans/jayco-tourer-2-berth-campervan/). It was a brand new build on the Renault base but has a different layout. The permanent bed set up was a big positive and other than a wet bath, was noted to have the same options as the Escape. Because it was new, not a lot of photos so we sent emails to Let’s Go confirming some of the features. We decided with such a deal, we’d go for 3 months, spending a month in Tassie before heading over to the mainland but debated where to go. We’ve almost done the whole “Big Lap” so thought about Perth to Darwin but weather wise it wasn’t the best idea. Autumn in Australia is supposed to be some of the best weather and other than March in Tassie, we hadn’t experienced it. We decided to stick to the East Coast area, we love our beach walks, and choose to head from Melbourne to Cairns. Paul spent a day or two looking at flights and found some really nice “bidness class” ones that we could book using our mileage rewards.  We’d fly KL-SIN-PER-HBA and then CNS-SIN-BKK. We booked everything and started looking forward to another roadtrip. 

Everything came to halt when we asked for more info about the van and found out it didn’t actually have some advertised features, such as a microwave and TV. Doesn’t sound like a massive deal but we used both daily in our prior roadtrips, the TV as the computer screen and melted cheese people! This started a flurry of back and forth with Let’s Go and us trying to sort out other options. They were all “oops my bad” but nothing we can do but you can cancel for a full refund if you want, your choice. Really it was a pisser as we were already looking forward to it and even though using reward points, we’d lose a bit on the flights. In the end, we found a work around, we’d bring the monitor from Thailand for the TV and use the camp kitchens when possible. It meant travelling with a duffle, but we could work with that. After a lot of pushing, Let’s Go tossed in free propane refills and a toll pass. Overall not impressed with Let’s Go handling of the issue, especially for repeat customers, but we decided to continue our plans!

When we checked in for our KL-SIN flight, they let us know that we could get the earlier flight instead of having 3 hours in the lounge. We decided that would be good, give us longer in case of any delays and there was also a highly rated lounge. We did manage to get a quick stop in the KL lounge, long enough for Paul to scarf down some pasta while I had some snacks. The flight to SIN was smooth and short, we made it over to the lounge, which was really busy but also nice. We found a couple of seats, had a snack and went for a walk before catching the flight from SIN-PER. This was another good flight we landed in Perth quite early. We sat in the Virgin lounge for a bit, had some decent coffee and of course took advantage of the fact there was food. We had a few hours before the last flight to Hobart so we set up our Telsim sim cards, sent some emails and probably sat there looking stunned because neither of us had slept that much. The flight to Hobart was smooth and we had a nice meal. The steward at the front of the cabin was vigilant about the toilet being for business class use only and kept sending everyone back. I got a big apology when he told me economy toilets were at the back before recognizing he had recently given me coffee. We landed on time, but had quite the wait for luggage before heading to our hotel for the night. We went for a short walk to get some air, got ourselves some Subway for dinner and went to sleep. 

The next morning, Rob from Tassie Motor Shacks, Let’s Go depot for the van, picked us up and within 30 minutes we were on our way. Jo was busy, but we still got to say hi. They had told us about a good breakfast place nearby so we headed over to come up with our plan of attack for stocking up the van before heading out to Left of Field. Good thing we did, because it was delicious and also how we found our favorite coffee for the whole trip. We managed to get everything sorted and by late that afternoon we were unpacked, hugs exchanged and enjoying being around the firepit at L.O.F. It was great to be back!

We spent the next few weeks walking around Mt. Field National Park, just crossing over the river and hitting up the 3 Falls trek several times. We finally figured out the short cut that removed about 200 stairs… thanks Mel! 🤗 Adrian, Mel and the kids, Craig, Bill, Nev, Paul(the singer) and Mark were all there at various times. There was live music with Drew Blundell, which was really good. Drew played another night with a fiddle player called Pixie Jenkins, who was also awesome. We had a couple of quieter nights with Tassie Paul singing. Our last night was a free concert by 8 Ball Aitkin and Tanya, which was excellent. There was roast lamb, sausages, sushi shared around the firepit and I made Pad Kra Pao one night. We had lots of interesting, and occasionally weird, conversations around the fire while tasty beverages were consumed. It was really great to just relax with everyone.

We wanted to do some longer hikes so booked a stay at Port Arthur and then some time in Cradle Mountain. Port Arthur was great, we stayed back at the NRMA site and made plans to hike Cape Raoul the next day. We had a hot and sunny day for the hike and it was lovely. The path had changed a bit, not surprising since it’s 9 years since we last did the hike, with more stairs and switchbacks being added. Apparently you can now do a 3 night tour with overnight guesthouses and boat rides to the next trailhead so the whole area has been upgraded. It was a nice day and while we were both a bit tired, we really enjoyed the hike. On the way back, we randomly stopped at one store and Paul found Moo Brew, an IPA that he really enjoyed. The next day we were supposed to head to Cradle Mountain, but the weather didn’t co-operate and it was raining for the next 3 days. It wasn't a hard decision to head back to L.O.F. for our last week in Tasmania.

We spent our last night at the Big 4 in Hobart. Our flight to Melbourne was fairly early and we had to clean, fuel up and drop off the van so we figured it was the best option. We managed to get everything done, including a stop at New Norfolk on the way to hit up the White Wallaby for laundry and some lunch so we could relax that evening.  We dropped off the van bright and early and Rob dropped us at the airport with time to get a coffee. We’ve both already said Tassie 2027 and we'll come back for longer. Hopefully the weather will co-operate and we’ll head to Cradle Mountain and possibly the [“Edge of the World”](https://tasmania.com/points-of-interest/arthur-river/) .
